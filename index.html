Research Interests
Architectural Robustness in NLP
I am collaborating with Nicholas Andrews at Johns Hopkins on understanding why recurrent architectures handle noisy input better than convolutional ones. We are running systematic ablation studies on language identification across 200 languages, testing gating mechanisms, pooling strategies, and parameter scaling. Early results: the robustness gap comes from gating, not pooling or model size. Recurrence itself provides a modest benefit, but gated recurrence (GRU, LSTM) is where the real gains are.
Reasoning Models for Event Prediction
I am interested in training reasoning models that can make calibrated predictions about real-world events: election outcomes, drug interaction likelihoods, clinical trial results. The question I keep coming back to is whether you can teach a model to reason over heterogeneous evidence (polling data, molecular structure, trial metadata) and produce well-calibrated probability estimates rather than just classification labels. This is early-stage thinking, but it is where I want to spend more time.
